04

Guardrails

How to use Cube responsibly, and why we don't go around it
Read first · Semantic layer guardrails

Use the semantic layer. Don't reinvent the joins.

Why Cube exists, what it costs us when queries get hand-rolled against the marts in BI tools or notebooks, and what to do when Cube can't answer your question. The single most important page in this center if you ever touch our data tooling.

Read the guardrails →

✓Day-to-day do's

  • Reference fields and values explicitly when you know them. Use real schema names.
  • Specify the time field, granularity, and range every time. Time errors are the most common.
  • Define the metric calculation. "Total revenue defined as sum of order_amount" beats "revenue."
  • If you don't know a field name, describe what it represents and give example values.
  • Treat prompting as iterative. Refine, don't restart, when a result looks off.
  • Cite the source. Cube tells you which data domains it pulled from. Look at it.

✕Day-to-day don'ts

  • Don't ask conversationally. "How is revenue doing recently?" is too vague to map cleanly.
  • Don't assume Cube knows your business definitions. Spell out "active users," "completed," etc.
  • Don't use Cube for PHI lookups about a specific identifiable member. Use approved tools.
  • Don't paste a Cube number into a board-level deck without checking the underlying query.
  • Don't write custom SQL against the marts in any tool. See why →
  • Don't share Cube outputs externally without going through the standard data review process.

The pattern, at a glance

The structure every strong prompt follows
Memorize this

Use this template every time

When you're not sure how to phrase your prompt, drop your question into these five slots first. The clearer you fill each one in, the more reliable the answer.

Show metric by dimension over time range, filtered to conditions, where definitions
Conversational · high risk How is revenue doing recently?
Structured · high confidence Show total revenue (sum of order_amount) by week over the last 8 weeks, where order_status = "completed"

FAQ & get help

Common questions answered, plus where to ask the rest
I asked Cube a simple question and got a weird answer. What now?

Open the prompting guide's troubleshooting section. Most "weird" answers fall into one of five buckets: wrong metric, wrong time granularity, missing filter, ambiguous business term, or a join-path issue. Refine, don't restart.

How do I know which domain my question lives in?

Start with the Cube Enterprise Agent. It will route your question to the right domain automatically. If you'd rather learn the landscape yourself, open the domain directory. Each card describes the building block the domain tracks (e.g. one appointment, one eligible person) so you can pick where to start.

Can I just write SQL against the marts to get what I need?

Please don't, unless it's an approved exception. It doesn't matter whether the SQL lives inside a BI tool, a notebook, an AI assistant, or a SQL client. Custom queries against our marts bypass the join logic, validation, and version control that Cube provides. They quietly create maintenance debt that surfaces later as broken reports and conflicting numbers. Read the semantic layer guardrails for the why and the alternative path.

Can I trust Cube numbers in a client-facing deck?

Treat Cube numbers as a fast first pass. For anything client-facing or board-facing, route through the standard data review process before publishing. The prompting guide also covers how to verify a result by checking which fields and joins were used.

Is it safe to look up an individual member in Cube?

Cube has row-level security enabled, just like Looker. If your role is approved to see that information, it's safe and you'll see it. If your role isn't approved, access is restricted automatically. The same governance you're used to in Looker carries over.

Can Cube integrate with Slack?

Yes. You can ask Cube questions directly from Slack and get answers back in-channel. Watch the walkthrough: Slack ↔ Cube Agent.

Where do I ask if my question isn't here?

While Cube is in UAT, drop a note in #proj-cube-uat with your prompt and the result you got back. Once we go live, support moves to #support-data-team. The team will route from there either way.
