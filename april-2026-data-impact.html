Operational incidents and root cause

  • Apr 13–14 · Airflow Resource Upgrade Outage. All Airflow DAGs began queueing on the morning of Apr 13 from resource exhaustion in our managed Airflow environment (MWAA). Root cause was infrastructure capacity, not pipeline logic. The MWAA environment was upgraded to xlarge and pipelines fully restored Apr 14. Stakeholder visibility, no client impact.
  • Apr 15 · Airflow dbt Log Accumulation. Same family of issue: dbt logs were accruing in the Airflow environment without a cleanup policy and exhausted disk. Logs cleaned up in the qg-airflow repo, the backend instance was upgraded to 2xlarge, and a cleanup policy is in place. Same-day resolution, limited visibility.
  • Apr 21 · Duplicate Guitar Center users in Iterable. A Guitar Center folder rename caused new eligibility_references to be generated; the prior records were never flipped to is_in_latest_file = false, so duplicate users persisted in Iterable and received outreach. Process-config issue, not a pipeline-integrity failure. P1 same-day mitigation, root-cause fix in flight (DE-6682 closed; tracked on the Iterable team).

One unique-key dbt test failure (DE-6682, billing span uniqueness) was caught and remediated before it reached billing reporting — a representative example of the test suite doing its job. Both Airflow incidents have follow-on platform hardening shipped (see "What you can act on") so the same conditions don't recur.
